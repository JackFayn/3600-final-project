Methodology

How the projection is built

We fit MW capacity and grid emission intensity each to an exponential, then multiply them. SciPy's curve_fit handles the nonlinear least-squares. Equations scroll horizontally if needed.

Capacity

MW(t) = a · eb(t−2021)

Emission intensity

E(t) = a · eb(t−2018)

Total CO2

CO2(t) = MW(t) · 8760 · E(t)

Both factors are exponential, so their product is too, with a growth rate equal to the sum of the two component rates.

Caveats

  • Only five years of MW data and six years of emission data per state, so the fitted growth rate has a wide margin of error.
  • The 2030 MW value comes from an industry forecast, not a measurement, and it has an outsized effect on the projection.
