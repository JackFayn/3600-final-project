Methodology

MW capacity and grid emission intensity are each fit to an exponential, then multiplied. SciPy's curve_fit runs the nonlinear least-squares. Equations scroll horizontally if needed.

Capacity

MW(t) = a · eb(t−2021)

Emission intensity

E(t) = a · eb(t−2018)

Total CO2

CO2(t) = MW(t) · 8760 · E(t)

Both factors are exponential, so their product is also exponential, with growth rate equal to the sum of the two component rates.

Caveats

  • Small sample: five MW points and six emission points per state. Confidence intervals on b are wide.
  • Assumes 100% utilization; real figures are lower, so results are an upper bound.
  • Exponential extrapolation; no saturation or floor is enforced.
  • The 2030 MW value is an industry projection, not an observation, and it pulls the fit.
